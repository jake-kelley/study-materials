How to Use These Guides

Each guide has four parts

  • High-level overview — orient fast; links jump into the deep sections.
  • Thorough study guide — every exam domain, decision tables, and service/command references.
  • Study materials — the most commonly recommended courses, practice exams & free resources, synthesized from Reddit & community write-ups (with sources).
  • Cram Jam — a dense, print-friendly one-pager to speed-read right before the exam.

Study for retention, not just the pass

  • Active recall > re-reading — quiz yourself, use flashcards, do practice exams early.
  • Spaced repetition — short daily sessions with growing review intervals beat cramming.
  • Interleaving — mix topics/question types the way the real exam does.
  • Hands-on + teach-back (Feynman) — build it and explain it out loud to expose gaps.
  • Sleep — memory consolidates overnight; never all-nighter the exam.
